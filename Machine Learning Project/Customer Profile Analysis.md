# Customer Cluster Personas

A detailed overview of customer segments to guide marketing, engagement, and retention strategies.

---

| **Cluster Name** | **Profile Summary** | **Key Characteristics** | **Business Insight** | **Recommended Strategy** |
|------------------|---------------------|-------------------------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| **Cluster 0<br>Affluent Loyalists** | The company's most valuable customers. High spenders, frequent purchasers, and extremely responsive to marketing campaigns. | • High income<br>• Small households (singles, couples, or one child)<br>• Middle-aged to older adults<br>• Long-term, loyal customers<br>• Highest total purchase value and purchase frequency<br>• Most responsive to marketing campaigns<br>• Very recent purchasers | This cluster drives maximum revenue and campaign ROI. | • Premium and personalized offers<br>• Loyalty rewards and early-access campaigns<br>• Cross-sell and upsell high-margin products |
| **Cluster 1<br>Budget-Conscious Families** | Large, family-oriented households with the lowest income and spending. Low engagement with campaigns and infrequent purchases. | • Lowest income<br>• Large families (3+ members)<br>• Middle-aged customers<br>• Long-term customers but low engagement<br>• Lowest spending and purchase frequency<br>• Least responsive to campaigns<br>• Moderate recency | This segment is price-sensitive and less responsive to traditional promotions. | • Value-based bundles and discounts<br>• Family-oriented promotions<br>• Essentials-focused product messaging |
| **Cluster 2<br>Established Traditionalists** | Older, family-based customers with moderate spending and engagement patterns. | • Medium income<br>• Large families with children<br>• Older customers<br>• Medium customer tenure<br>• Moderate spending and purchase frequency<br>• Moderate campaign acceptance<br>• Moderate recency | This cluster offers growth potential with the right incentives. | • Targeted promotions and seasonal campaigns<br>• Product recommendations based on past purchases<br>• Gradual upsell strategies |
| **Cluster 3<br>Selective High Earners** | High-income customers who purchase frequently but show lower campaign engagement and longer gaps between purchases. | • High income (below Cluster 0)<br>• Mostly singles and couples<br>• Mixed age group (younger to older, including empty nesters)<br>• Relatively newer customers<br>• High purchase frequency<br>• Selective campaign engagement<br>• Long time since last purchase | These customers have high earning potential but weaker retention. | • Re-engagement and win-back campaigns<br>• Personalized messaging rather than mass promotions<br>• Incentives focused on convenience and exclusivity |

## Executive Takeaway

| **Cluster** | **Strategic Focus** |
|-------------|---------------------|
| **Cluster 0 — Affluent Loyalists** | Protect and maximize value |
| **Cluster 1 — Budget-Conscious Families** | Engage through affordability |
| **Cluster 2 — Established Traditionalists** | Nurture and grow |
| **Cluster 3 — Selective High Earners** | Re-activate and retain |
